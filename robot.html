Our Robot

Robot Components

Our robot is unique and innovative, as it has undergone countless redesigns and replacements. It consists of:

  • An arm attached to a servo to elevate balls into the flywheel
  • Hooded shooter and delivery mechanism
  • Two rows of wheels run by a motor and a chain to intake artifacts
  • A ramp for artifacts to roll into the inner transition area
  • An arch to help align artifacts during the transition area

Flywheel

The 3D-printed hooded shooter, designed in Fusion 360, receives the ball directly from the intake system. Fast scoring is crucial, so we optimized the design for speed and accuracy.

  • FIRST Version: Added length for proper shooting angle
  • SECOND Version: Adjusted proportions, reduced sharp angles
  • THIRD Version (Final): Optimized angle; hood length for consistent shots

Intake System

The intake system guides balls into the robot. Improvements over time ensured stability, speed, and alignment. Ramp reflects the team colors.

Team Number Plate

We 3D-printed the team plate to ensure it stays in place during matches. Current version uses Velcro strips.

CAD Designs & Build

We progressed from sketches to CAD models to the final physical build. Each iteration refined intake, outtake, and structure for optimal performance.

Cable Management

Zip-ties keep wires organized and away from wheels and moving parts, ensuring smooth operation.
