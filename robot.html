Robot Components

Our robot is unique and innovative, as it has undergone countless redesigns and replacements. Learning from past mistakes, we designed a creative robot that consists of:

  • An arm attached to a servo to elevate balls into the flywheel
  • Hooded shooter and delivery mechanism
  • Two rows of wheels run by a motor and a chain to intake artifacts
  • A ramp for artifacts to roll into the inner transition area
  • An arch to help align artifacts during the transition area

Regular components include:

  • 6 motors for outtake, intake, and 4 Omni wheels
  • 1 big servo
  • Camera for April tags
  • Zip ties for wire management
  • Control hub and expansion hub

Robot Design - Flywheel

The 3D-printed hooded shooter, designed in Fusion 360, receives the ball directly from the intake system. Fast scoring is crucial, so we optimized the design for speed and accuracy.

Flywheel Improvements:

  • FIRST Version: Added length for proper shooting angle; learned geometry affects trajectory
  • SECOND Version: Adjusted proportions; reduced sharp angles to prevent strain
  • THIRD Version (Final Iteration): Optimized angle; hood length set for consistent shots

Robot Design - Intake System (Ramp & Wheels)

The intake system guides balls smoothly into the robot. Improvements over time ensured stability, speed, and alignment. The ramp reflects the team color palette.

Intake System Improvements:

  • FIRST Version: Adjusted ramp angle; wheels lacked grip
  • SECOND Version: Strengthened ramp; swapped to better wheels
  • THIRD Version (Final Iteration): Optimized wheel spacing; extended ramp for consistent feed
